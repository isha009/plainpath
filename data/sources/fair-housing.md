# Fair Housing — Protection from Discrimination
> Source: U.S. Dept. of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), Fair Housing Act overview — https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp/fair_housing_act_overview

## Protected classes
The federal Fair Housing Act prohibits discrimination in housing because of race, color, national origin, religion, sex (including gender identity and sexual orientation), familial status (having children under 18), and disability. These are called "protected classes." State and local laws sometimes add more protected classes, such as source of income, age, or marital status.

## What counts as discrimination
Prohibited conduct includes refusing to rent or sell, setting different terms or conditions, falsely denying that housing is available, and steering people toward or away from certain buildings or neighborhoods because of a protected class. Harassment because of a protected class is also prohibited.

## Disability accommodations
A landlord must allow reasonable accommodations (changes to rules, policies, or services) and reasonable modifications (physical changes) when needed for a person with a disability to use the housing. Example: allowing a service or assistance animal despite a no-pets policy. The tenant may be responsible for the cost of physical modifications in private housing.

## How to file a complaint
A person who believes they experienced housing discrimination can file a complaint with HUD, generally within one year of the incident, at no cost. HUD investigates and may refer matters for enforcement. Filing a complaint is separate from any lawsuit and does not require a lawyer.
